## Part 2: The Compliance Question (Separating Concern from Crisis)

### What SEC Regulation S-P Actually Requires

**From Your Research:**
> "SEC Regulation S-P requires financial institutions to protect customer records and information. It limits how and when institutions can disclose consumer information and mandates written policies to safeguard customer data."

**Key Requirements:**
1. **Safeguards Rule**: Implement administrative, technical, physical safeguards for customer info
2. **Privacy Notice**: Provide clear privacy policies to customers
3. **Opt-Out Rights**: Allow customers to opt out of information sharing
4. **Data Breach Notification**: Report breaches to SEC within specific timeframes

**Critical Question:** What constitutes "customer financial information"?

**Definitions:**

**Definitely Covered (Must Be Protected):**
- Bank account numbers
- Credit card numbers
- Transaction details
- Account balances
- Investment information
- Loan details

**Gray Area (Depends on Context):**
- Names + email addresses (not financial info alone)
- Company names (business contact info, not financial)
- Lead source tracking (marketing attribution, not financial)
- Website behavior (analytics, not financial)

**Marketing Operations Context:**

**What's Typically in HubSpot (Compliant):**
- Contact information: Name, email, company, title
- Marketing engagement: Email opens, clicks, website visits
- Lead scoring: Behavioral + demographic data
- Company information: Industry, size, revenue range
- Form submissions: Demo requests, content downloads

**What Should NOT Be in HubSpot (Non-Compliant):**
- Customer bank account details
- Transaction history
- Account balances
- Credit card information
- Individual financial data

**The Critical Question You Need to Answer:**
1. Is Rho storing ACTUAL financial data in HubSpot (account numbers, balances, transactions)?
2. OR is HubSpot used only for marketing data (contact info, engagement, lead scoring)?

**If #2 (Marketing Data Only):**
- This is COMPLIANT and standard practice for fintech companies
- SEC Regulation S-P doesn't prohibit marketing automation platforms
- Your research question was preventative/educational, not crisis response

**If #1 (Financial Data in HubSpot):**
- This is NON-COMPLIANT and serious violation
- Requires immediate escalation to legal/compliance team
- Document in writing, create paper trail, exit if not resolved
