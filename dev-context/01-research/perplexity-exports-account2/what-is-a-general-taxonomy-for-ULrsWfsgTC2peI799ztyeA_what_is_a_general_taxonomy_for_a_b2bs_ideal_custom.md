## General Taxonomy for a B2B Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

A B2B Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) is a structured description of the company or organization that is the best fit for your product or service, maximizing the likelihood of successful sales, high customer value, and long-term retention[^1][^5][^6]. The taxonomy for a B2B ICP typically includes several key categories:

**Firmographics**

- Industry/Sector: The primary industry or vertical (e.g., SaaS, manufacturing, healthcare)[^4][^5][^6][^7][^9].
- Company Size: Usually measured by number of employees or annual revenue[^4][^5][^6][^7][^9].
- Location: Geographic region, country, or specific markets served[^4][^5][^6][^7][^9].
- Business Model: B2B, B2C, or hybrid; public vs. private; single vs. multi-location[^8][^9].
- Growth Stage: Startup, scale-up, established enterprise, or declining business[^5][^9].

**Financial Attributes**

- Revenue/Budget: Annual revenue, purchasing power, or available budget for your solution[^6][^7][^9].
- Profitability: Financial health and ability to invest in new solutions[^5][^6].

**Operational Characteristics**

- Technology Stack: Existing tools, platforms, or systems in use—especially relevant for tech products[^6][^9].
- Number of Locations/Subsidiaries: Single-site vs. multi-site operations, global presence[^9].
- Customer Base Size: Size and type of their own customers, relevant for solutions that scale with their client base[^6].

**Needs \& Pain Points**

- Business Challenges: Specific problems your solution addresses (e.g., cost reduction, efficiency, compliance)[^4][^5][^7].
- Urgency: Readiness to buy and urgency of their need[^5].
- Goals \& Objectives: Growth plans, digital transformation, or other strategic priorities[^5][^7].

**Behavioral \& Psychographic Factors**

- Buying Process: Length and complexity of their purchasing cycle, presence of a buying committee[^7][^9].
- Decision-Making Criteria: What matters most (price, ROI, features, support, etc.)[^7].
- Values \& Culture: Openness to innovation, risk tolerance, or alignment with your brand’s values[^7].

**Other Considerations**

- Legal/Conflict Restrictions: Any conflicts of interest or exclusivity concerns[^6].
- Compatibility: Ability to integrate with their systems or processes[^6].
- Referenceability: Likelihood to serve as a reference or case study[^5].

## Summary

A general taxonomy for a B2B ICP includes firmographics, financials, operational details, needs and pain points, behavioral/psychographic factors, and other relevant considerations. This structure helps B2B organizations focus sales and marketing efforts on accounts with the highest potential for value and success[^1][^4][^5][^6][^7][^9].
