## SECTION 33: WHAT NOW - PAGE 2

**Heading:** Start With Roles

**Content:**

**The Alignment Audit (Do This Tomorrow):**

**Step 1: Pull three documents**
1. The job description you used to hire your MOps team
2. The goals you set in their last 1:1 or performance review
3. Their actual work log for one week (ask them to track it)

**Step 2: Compare them**

Put them side by side.

Look for gaps:
- Job description says "lifecycle" but goals say "MQL volume"
- Job description says "strategic" but work log shows "list pulls"
- Job description says "architecture" but goals measure "campaign speed"

**Step 3: Quantify the misalignment**

Calculate the percentage of time spent on:
- Work that matches job description: ___%
- Work that matches goals: ___%
- Work that is neither (firefighting, workarounds): ___%

If those three numbers don't add up to 80%+ match, you have role misalignment.

**Step 4: Fix the role before fixing the tools**

**Don't do this:**
- Migrate to a new platform
- Hire a consultant
- Add more tools to the stack
- Reorganize the team structure

**Do this:**
- Rewrite the job description to match reality OR
- Change the goals to match the job description OR
- Change the work allocation to match both

**Pick one. Commit to it.**

**Example:**

**Scenario: Alex (Lifecycle MOps hired, Demand Gen goals assigned)**

**Option A: Rewrite Job as Demand Gen**
- New title: Demand Generation Coordinator
- New goals: MQL volume, list quality, campaign speed

- Keep title: Marketing Operations Manager
- New goals: Nurture conversion rates, lifecycle stage velocity, customer retention
- Remove: MQL volume targets (move to demand gen team)
- Outcome: Alex can finally do the job they were hired for

**Option C: Hire Both Roles**
- Keep Alex as Lifecycle MOps
